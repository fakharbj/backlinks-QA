# LinkSentinel — What's New (Plain‑English Guide)

> A simple guide to the next big upgrade — written for everyone, no technical
> background needed. For each feature you'll see: **what it is**, **a short story
> of how you'd use it**, and **what you get** out of it.

---

## The big picture (in one minute)

Today LinkSentinel checks your backlinks one by one: is the link still there? Does
it point to the right place? Is Google showing it? This upgrade makes the whole
system **smarter, more flexible, and able to find you new opportunities**:

1. **Set up each client project properly** — tell the system which website each
   project is about, who's working on it, and what "a good backlink" means *for
   that client*.
2. **Pull everything from your Google Sheets automatically** — including the
   different *kinds* of links (Web 2.0, Profile, Guest Post…), which live on
   separate tabs in your sheets.
3. **Understand your backlinks by the websites they live on** — not just one link
   at a time, but "show me everything about facebook.com / that blog / that forum."
4. **Spy on competitors (the legal, smart way)** — upload a competitor's backlink
   list and instantly see which of *their* link sources you already use and which
   are **brand‑new opportunities** your team should go after.
5. **Slice, filter, and report on absolutely everything** — every number, status,
   score, and category can be filtered and put into a report or sent to Google
   Sheets.

Think of it as going from *"a link checker"* to *"a control room for your entire
off‑page SEO operation."*

**Who benefits:**
- 🧑‍💼 **Agency owner / manager** — oversight, clean reports, accountability.
- 🔍 **SEO / QA team** — faster checking, clear quality scores, fewer mistakes.
- 🚀 **Off‑page / link‑building team** — a constant supply of new link
  opportunities.
- 👤 **Your clients** — professional, branded reports that prove the work.

---

## 1. Set up each project the right way (Project Settings + Main Website)

**In plain words:** Each project gets a proper settings page. The most important
setting is the **client's main website** — the site you're building backlinks
*to*. Once the system knows it, every check, report, and chart is automatically
about *that* website.

**Your story:** *"As an account manager, I want to set my client's website once,
so the whole system automatically knows what every backlink should point to — no
re‑typing it for every link."*

**What you get:**
- One place to manage each project's basics.
- The system always checks links against the **right** target website.
- Change it safely — your past records stay intact and accurate.

---

## 2. Know exactly who did what (Team & Employee Codes)

**In plain words:** Add and manage your team members and their **employee codes**,
and connect the names that appear in your Google Sheets to real people in the
system.

**Your story:** *"As a manager, I want each backlink tied to the real team member
who built it, so I can see everyone's work and run reports per person."*

**What you get:**
- Add/edit team members and assign employee codes yourself.
- The names from your sheets are matched to real people automatically (you confirm
  the matches).
- Clean **"work done per person"** reports — great for performance reviews and
  client billing.

---

## 3. Decide what "a good backlink" means — your rules (Quality Scoring)

**In plain words:** Right now every backlink gets a health score using one fixed
formula. This upgrade lets **you** decide how scoring works — overall, and
differently for each *type* of link, and even differently for each client.

**Your story:** *"As an SEO lead, I want a 'no‑follow' Web 2.0 link to count as
fine, but a 'no‑follow' guest post to count as a problem — because they're not the
same thing. And for my biggest client, I want stricter rules."*

**What you get:**
- Set **default scoring rules** for each link type (Web 2.0, Profile, Guest
  Post…).
- **Override** those rules for any specific client when needed.
- Scores reflect *your* standards, not a one‑size‑fits‑all formula.
- When you change the rules, **old reports stay exactly as they were** — history is
  never quietly rewritten.

---

## 4. Pull the right data from your Google Sheets (Link Types from Tabs)

**In plain words:** Your project sheets keep different link types on different
tabs (Web 2.0, Profile, Forum…). The system will read all those tabs and let you
**tick boxes** to choose which tabs to import, which to check, and which to ignore.

**Your story:** *"As a team lead, I want to import only the tabs that matter and
skip the ones that are work‑in‑progress, with a simple checklist."*

**What you get:**
- The system finds every tab in your sheet automatically.
- Simple **checkboxes**: import this tab? check it? which link type is it?
- Renaming or adding a tab won't break anything — the system handles it gracefully.
- Each backlink is automatically tagged with its correct link type.

---

## 5. Delete things safely — never lose work by accident (Safe Delete)

**In plain words:** Before anything is deleted (a project, a sheet, a user…), the
system **shows you what's connected** and asks you to confirm. Deleted items go to
a "recently deleted" area first, so you can undo.

**Your story:** *"As an admin, I want a clear warning — 'this project has 4,000
links and 3 reports' — before I delete it, and a way to get it back if I made a
mistake."*

**What you get:**
- Clear "here's what will be affected" warnings before deleting.
- A **restore** option for a grace period — accidental deletes are recoverable.
- A full record of who deleted what and when.

---

## 6. Understand backlinks by the website they're on (Source‑Website View)

**In plain words:** Instead of scrolling through thousands of individual links, you
get a view **grouped by the website each backlink lives on** — e.g. everything from
`facebook.com`, that one blog, that forum.

**Your story:** *"As an SEO analyst, I want to click 'medium.com' and instantly see
how many links we have there, how many Google is showing, what types they are, and
how strong that site is."*

**What you get:**
- A dashboard of every **source website**, with totals, indexed %, link types,
  assigned people, average score, and more.
- Spot your **best link sources** at a glance.
- See where you're under‑using a strong website (room to build more).

---

## 7. Know how strong each website is (Website Authority Score)

**In plain words:** For every website your backlinks live on, the system fetches an
**authority score** (how strong/trustworthy that site is) and remembers it.

**Your story:** *"As a link builder, I want to focus my time on high‑authority
websites, so I want that strength score shown right next to every source."*

**What you get:**
- An authority/strength score for each source website, kept up to date.
- See authority trends over time.
- Prioritise effort on the sites that actually move the needle.

> Note: the system can use **Moz (DA/PA)** or **Similarweb** for this — your choice.
> (Currently set to Similarweb on the server; we'll switch to Moz if you prefer.)

---

## 8. Find new backlink opportunities from competitors (Competitor Analysis)

This is the headline feature for your link‑building team.

**In plain words:** Upload a competitor's backlink list (a sheet) into a project.
The system reads where the competitor gets *their* backlinks, then compares it to
where *you* already have links — and splits the result into two buckets:

- ✅ **Websites you already use** — with advice like "you have only 1 link here but
  it's a strong site — get more."
- 🌟 **Brand‑new opportunity websites** — places the competitor uses that **you
  don't** — ready for your team to go after.

It even **guesses the link type** for new opportunities (e.g. "this is a Facebook
URL and we treat Facebook as Profile links, so this is probably a Profile
opportunity"), and flags anything unclear for a human to check.

**Your story:** *"As the off‑page team lead, I want to drop in a competitor's
backlink sheet and get a ready‑to‑work list of new websites we should build links
on, sorted by how valuable they are."*

**What you get:**
- A clear **"existing vs. new"** breakdown for every competitor source.
- A ranked list of **new opportunities** (by authority, relevance, and value).
- Auto‑suggested link type for each opportunity (you can override).
- A simple **work board** for the off‑page team: assign opportunities, move them
  from "to do" → "in progress" → "done."
- Competitor data is kept **completely separate** from your real backlink data —
  no mixing, no confusion.

---

## 9. Slice, filter, and report on EVERYTHING (Dynamic Filters & Reports)

**In plain words:** Every piece of information in the system — status, score, link
type, website, authority, person, client, indexed or not, duplicate or not,
competitor category, opportunity value — can be **filtered** and put into a
**report**. Nothing is "view‑only."

**Your story:** *"As a manager, I want to ask things like 'show me all no‑follow
guest‑post links, on websites with authority above 40, that Google isn't showing,
for Client X' — and then export that exact list as a report or to a Google Sheet."*

**What you get:**
- Powerful filters on **any** field, with live counts as you narrow down.
- Build a report from **any** filter, choose **which columns** you want, and export
  to Excel, CSV, PDF, or **straight back into a Google Sheet**.
- Save your favourite filters and report setups to reuse in one click.
- Reports are **frozen snapshots** — what you sent the client last month stays
  exactly as it was.

---

## 10. Full history and accountability (Activity & Audit Log)

**In plain words:** The system keeps a record of important changes — who edited
settings, who changed a score rule, who deleted something — so there's always a
clear trail.

**Your story:** *"As an owner, I want to see who changed what, so the team is
accountable and nothing happens silently."*

**What you get:**
- A searchable history of key actions and changes.
- Confidence that nothing important happens without a record.

## So… what do you get overall?

| Before | After this upgrade |
|---|---|
| Check links one by one | Understand backlinks **by website, by type, by person, by client** |
| One fixed quality formula | **Your own** quality rules, per link type and per client |
| Manual sheet handling | Automatic import of the **right tabs**, tagged by link type |
| No competitor insight | Upload a competitor sheet → **instant list of new opportunities** |
| Limited filtering/reports | **Filter and report on everything**, export to Excel/PDF/Google Sheets |
| Risky deletes | **Safe deletes** with warnings and undo |

**In one line:** you move from *checking backlinks* to **running your entire
off‑page SEO operation from one place** — with your own quality standards, a steady
stream of competitor‑driven opportunities, and professional reports you can put in
front of any client.
